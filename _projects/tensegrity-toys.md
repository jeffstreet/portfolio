It’s difficult to build small tensegrities with household materials, and there isn’t a construction set that allows rapid experimentation. Lego bricks, Erector sets, and Kinects are great ways to build intuition about how structures behave in the real world.

I’ve been wrestling with this problem for a few years, and finally hit on a solution in an unexpected place: cheap menus. The shock cord barb system they use is perfect for quickly and easily linking strings to struts.
