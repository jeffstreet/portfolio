[Tensegrity](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tensegrity) is a strategy of structural design. Compression elements (rods) and tension elements (strings) are combined to create low density structures capable of carrying impressive loads. These models were built with the same materials that [Kenneth Snelson](http://kennethsnelson.net/) used in 1950s: aluminum tubes and ball chain. There isn’t anything better available commercially. So, I’m toying with the idea of developing a tensegrity building kit to enable freeform experimentation with tensegrity structures.
