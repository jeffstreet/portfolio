Here’s a concept sketch of a large-scale pegboard for cutting with a cnc router.

The material is plywood, ¾ inch (18mm) thick. Slots are ¾ x 2 inches (18 x 50mm), centered on a 6 inch (150mm) grid. The board shown is 48 inches (1220 mm) wide. 

The attachments are (from top left): hook, towel rod, shelf 6 inches deep, shelf 12 inches deep, clothes hanger rod, shelf 18 inches deep. Shelves have slots cut in the bottom that register with retaining pins cut into the shelf supports. Rods are steel pipe, ¾ inch trade, 1.1 inch diameter.

This concept addresses shortcomings of other large-scale pegboard construction methods, like 1 inch wood dowels in rough holes. The dowels are retained by the friction fit with their holes. This interface can’t support much torque and so loosens over time, allowing the dowels to tilt downward. Further, dowels don’t offer a mechanism to retain shelves or to mount a bar parallel to the pegboard surface.

I’m thinking about changing from dogbone slots to rounded rectangle slots that are slightly wider because I don’t like the look of dogbones. I don’t think there’s a downside to this because none of the attachments sketched here need precise location of their brackets. There may be an upside too because ovals will allow for thickness variation of the plywood.
