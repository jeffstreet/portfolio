Goal: Design a 48 inch long scale model of the J-class sailing yacht [Endeavour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endeavour_(yacht))  and the fixtures/jigs needed to build it.

Background:

- The model, hull and deck, will be built with strips of wood,  3 x 6 mm in cross-section.
- Example photo of stick boat building.

Requirements:

- Frame for hull and deck must be removable after strips have been glued together.
- Keel should be extended 3 inches downward from the scale depth.

Open Questions:

- Frame material: Type? Thickness? Sheet size?
- Frame material cutter: Machine type? Max sheet size?

Deliverables:

- Frame for hull strips. Series of cross-sectional ribs that the strips can be formed over, and a structure to align/support the ribs during construction.
- Frame for deck strips. Series of cross-sectional ribs that the strips can be formed over, and a structure to align/support the ribs during construction.
- Brackets for mounting deck to hull.
- Brackets for internal components (rudder, servos, battery).
- Template for rudder.
