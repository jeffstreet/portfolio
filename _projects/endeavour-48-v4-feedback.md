We reviewed the drawings when I visited home for Christmas. There were a few mistakes, and we made a few decisions about the next steps.

We decided that the surface in these drawings will represent the hull’s inner surface, rather than the hull’s outer surface. The reason is that the lumber selected for this project (3 x 6 mm cross-section) won’t permit building the curves that would be formed if 3mm were subtracted from the surface. The keel in particular becomes too thin.

We established the rudder outline and sketched the rudder hardware.

A couple open questions remain:

- Cutting machine (i.e. laser or router)
- How will the frame bottoms be secured during construction? The scheme shown on [Version 1](http://jeffstreet.com/post/103802593357/endeavour48-v1) doesn’t seem substantial enough.
