After the Brexit vote, people in the UK started wearing safety pins as a symbol of solidarity with immigrants and minorities. We need something like this in the United States. [Jason Kottke writes](http://kottke.org/16/11/safety-pins-as-a-symbol-of-solidarity-against-racism):

> I like this idea, that a subtle marker can denote a social safe space of sorts, a signal to someone who might feel uncomfortable that an ally is nearby. That’s not to say you can put a pin on your coat and *dust off your hands, job well done* but it may help. I’m going to try it.

Me too. And if you don’t have a safety pin handy, this photo could be your phone wallpaper ([full-res file](https://www.dropbox.com/s/pl6aqf29mh92s59/Safety%20pin%20heart.jpg?dl=0)).
