**Camino Apartment, Redondo Beach 
---
Village**

This apartment was my home for 3 years. When I moved to Paris, I decided it would be a hassle to haggle with craigslisters over each individual piece of furniture, so I sold it as a lot to the next tenant. It was fun to do a photoshoot ([thanks Susie](http://www.susiedelaney.com/)), and interview applicants.

Here’s the ad copy I used:

I’m moving to Paris and need to sell all my furniture. You’re moving to the South Bay and need to buy new furniture. That’s a lot of work. Instead, buy mine at a steep discount and move into this apartment. It’s got everything you need!

---

It’s a top floor unit with big picture windows and lofted ceiling. The deck is huge, and I’ve got it set up as an outdoor living room. If you’re 5'10" or taller, there’s an ocean view!

It’s got a private garage, which is a must in the summertime because street parking gets crowded. My car and motorcycle fit comfortably, along with a SUP, bicycle, and misc tools/storage.

The neighborhood is great. You’re a block from the beach and a half a block from Catalina street, the heart of Riviera Village. There are a several good bars: go to Redondo Beach Brewing for the food, Sophie’s for the beer, and there’s a new Rock & Brews too. Over at HT Grill they have a firepit and a good wine selection. Turquoise is the best restaurant in the neighborhood. There are three grocery stores: Von’s is one block away, Trader Joe’s is three, and Albertson’s is about five.

---

It’s fully furnished with modern electronics and mid-century pieces. Here are some of the highlights:

- 120" Projector with Apple TV
- Leather couch and club chair
- Fully equipped kitchen, with service for 8
- Stainless steel propane BBQ
- Queen pillow top bed
- Meyer lemon tree (delicious lemonade)

Literally everything else in the pictures is included. Come by and see it in person.
