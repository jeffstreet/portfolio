I’m just about to turn 5 years old. Literally, the next thing on the home movie tape is a closeup of me donning a birthday crown.

My uncle is a pilot. In 1987 he flew an F-18 for the Marines. Somehow, he had arranged to fly in to Boeing Field in Renton, Washington and stay the night. The whole family was out on the tarmac to see him arrive.

Watching it now, there’s an aspect of routine to the whole thing. He landed, taxied over to us, and parked between a private plane and a sightseeing helicopter. Just a guy who flew his plane home to visit his family.

It was cool. A real fighter jet! And people did come over to look at it, but in the late 80s the idea of a military jet landing at a civilian airport wasn’t that far out of the ordinary. There was no extra security. They didn’t whisk it away and hide it in a special hanger. The plane just sat there overnight. No perimeter. No armed guard. It just sat there. Everything was so relaxed.

As a kid, I didn’t see any of that. It was my birthday, and I got to sit in the cockpit of a no-kidding fighter jet. He probably even let me fire some missiles. That experience is one of my best childhood memories. Thanks again, Uncle Tom.
