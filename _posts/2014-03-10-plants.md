I like gardening because plants invite thinking on several time-scales.

Plants are dynamic. They continually change. When you buy a plant, what you’re purchasing is both its history and its potential. Someone cared for it to make it what it is today. And by doing the same, I will shape it into what it will be in the future.

Plants need attention regularly. Each day is an opportunity to give it some water, or give it some food, or remove a pest, or prune a dead limb, or just let it grow.

Bonsai are an extreme example of this. I have a tiny juniper. It’s in a tiny pot, so it’s important to water it every day. But it grows very slowly, and to care for it properly is to pinch it back, so it grows even more slowly. To make a beautiful bonsai, you need to have a long-term plan. To choose the dominant branches, and maybe train them to improve their positions.
