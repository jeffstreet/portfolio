This Honda S2000 was my daily driver for 6 years. I loved it, but had to sell before moving to Paris. It was fun to have it detailed ([thanks Nic](http://eye4detail.com/index.html)), do a photoshoot ([thanks Julia](http://www.julialapalme.com)), and write the ad. The selling price (2014) ended up being $1,000 above the purchase price (2008)!

Here’s the ad copy:

> Want a sweet cat-back exhaust on stanced suspension? Want three-piece rims, crazy camber, and rubber band tires? Want a trunk full of subwoofers? Not here!

> But if you’re looking for a pristine example of the S2000, you’ve found it. I’ve done my absolute best over the past 6 years of ownership to keep this beauty completely stock. Why mess with perfection?

> There are a lot of salvage titled S2000s out there, is this one of them? No way! Clean CarFax in hand.

> How’s the engine? Very strong, and purring like a kitten after a belly rub. I do all my own work, and have followed the maintenance schedule religiously. The Honda Service manual is included, along with several special tools you’ll want.

> How’s the paint? In great shape. It gets professionally detailed twice a year.

> Is the cheap OEM steering wheel leather flaking? Both the steering wheel and the shifter were professionally recovered with lambskin. Bet you didn’t notice! Go back and check the pictures. It looks stock, feels much better, and won’t flake.

> Any yellowing in the headlights and taillights like other S2000s? Nope. She lives most of her life in a parking garage so her headlights and taillights are as sparkling as the day she was new.

> How many holes does the top have? None! The top is original, and is in excellent condition. I’ve even replaced the elastic with the [Modifry elastic kit](http://modifry.com/products/ctf/index.htm), so you don’t have to worry about it deteriorating. Looks and works as stock.

> Is the phone mount included? Yes. It’s also [from Modifry](http://modifry.com/products/mounts/mdb.htm), and mounts with stock bolts. It will fit any phone. If you’ve got an iPod or and iPhone, there’s an adapter to connect to the stock stereo.

> Everything sounds perfect, are there any issues? Engine and body are pristine, and the tires have plenty of tread. There are only two minor things to note. The previous owner was hard on the driver’s outboard seat bolster. There are a couple small scratches in the paint, but I’ve had them professionally buffed.

> This is a great car. Why sell it? We’re moving to Paris, and I’d rather see this beauty driving around in the sunshine, than locked up in a storage unit.

> Whoa, isn’t $20k kind of high for a 2004 model? There are certainly a lot of cheaper S2000s for sale with salvage titles, 100K+ miles, and endless modifications thrown on. If you want to own a glorious high-revving, jaw dropping, constantly compliment receiving, gorgeous, bone stock, pristine S2000 … this is your car. This sort of beauty, in great condition and with low mileage, doesn’t come around very often. Plus, the value of these cars is actually rising, a pretty rare thing for a car so new. If you pass on this car, you’ll always regret it.

> Serious inquires please, and don’t expect a test drive without proof that you can buy. This means a bank statement or loan pre-approval. I know this sounds harsh, but I’m not handing out joyrides.
